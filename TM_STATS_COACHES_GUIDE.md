## Welcome

TM Stats gives you a data layer on top of your coaching. While you are with your players on the range or the course, you see their technique, their tendencies, their mental state. TM Stats captures what happens in actual rounds — where shots are genuinely lost, over time, under real playing conditions.

This guide walks you through everything: setting up your account, adding players, understanding the data, and using the AI Challenge to deliver coaching that combines your expertise with the platform's analysis.

## 1. Your First Login

Your login credentials (email and password) will be provided by TM Stats directly. No signup is required — your account is pre-created with Coach access already enabled.

**To log in:**
1. Go to **tmstatsgolf.com**
2. Tap **Log in**
3. Enter your email and password
4. You will land on your Coach Dashboard

**On your first visit**, you will be prompted to create your team. This takes 30 seconds — see Section 2.

---

## 2. Creating Your Team

Before players can join your squad, you need to create a team.

1. From your Coach Dashboard, tap **Create team**
2. Enter your team name (e.g., "Cheshire Academy", "John Smith Golf", "City Golf Club Juniors")
3. Tap **Create**

The system immediately generates your unique **8-character join code** (e.g., `MXQT7F2A`).

**This join code is how players link themselves to your squad.** Share it with your players — in a WhatsApp message, printed on a card, written on a whiteboard — however works for your setup. Players enter it in their Profile settings and they will appear in your squad instantly.

> **Note:** Your join code does not expire and does not change. You only need to create your team once.

## 3. Adding Players to Your Squad

**Your responsibility:** Share your join code with the players you coach.

**Player's responsibility:** Enter the code in the TM Stats app.

**How a player joins:**
1. They log in to TM Stats (or sign up for a free account)
2. They go to **Profile** (bottom navigation)
3. They scroll down to **Join a team**
4. They enter your 8-character join code and tap **Join**
5. They appear in your squad immediately

**Important:** Players join voluntarily. You cannot add a player without their action — this protects player data privacy. Players can also leave a team at any time from their Profile settings.

**Do players need to be on Pro?** No. Players can be on the Free plan and still be part of your squad. They can log rounds and you can view their stats. However, Free plan players are limited to 5 rounds. If they want unlimited round history, they will need to upgrade. TM Stats can provide promo codes for coaches to distribute — contact info@tmstatsgolf.com.

## 5. Viewing a Player's Stats

Tapping a player in your squad takes you to their player profile page. This shows:

### Strokes Gained Summary
If the player has logged full-tracking rounds (shot-by-shot entry), you will see their average Strokes Gained across all four categories:

| Category | What it measures |
|---|---|
| **SG: Off Tee** | Performance with the driver and long irons off tee boxes |
| **SG: Approach** | Iron and mid-game shots from outside 30 yards |
| **SG: Around Green** | Chipping, pitching, bunker play inside 30 yards |
| **SG: Putting** | Performance on the putting surface |

**How to read these numbers:**  
A positive number means the player is *gaining* shots against their benchmark. A negative number means they are *losing* shots. The benchmark is set against other players of their handicap level — not Tour professionals.

**Example:**  
A 12-handicap player shows:  
- SG: Off Tee +0.4 (gaining shots off the tee vs other 12-handicappers)  
- SG: Approach −0.8 (losing shots with irons vs other 12-handicappers)  
- SG: Around Green −0.2 (slight weakness around the green)  
- SG: Putting +0.1 (roughly benchmark level)

This player's primary coaching priority is clear: approach play.

### Recent Rounds
A list of the player's recent rounds with date, course, score relative to par, and SG total. Tap any round to see the full round detail — all hole data, shot-by-shot breakdown, SG per category, scoring distribution, and the AI feedback the player received.

## 6. Understanding Strokes Gained Data

Strokes Gained is the most powerful tool on the platform. Here is how to interpret it effectively as a coach.

### The Principle
SG measures how many shots were gained or lost *relative to benchmark* — not just what happened. This is important because context matters.

**Traditional stats problem:**  
A player hits 10 of 14 fairways. Is that good? It depends entirely on whether those 10 fairways translated into scoring opportunities, and whether the 4 misses were in play or in deep rough. FIR% doesn't tell you this. SG: Off Tee does.

**SG advantage:**  
A player with SG: Off Tee of +0.5 is gaining half a shot per round off the tee compared to similar-handicap players — regardless of what their FIR% looks like.

### Identifying True Weaknesses
Look for the category with the most negative SG value over multiple rounds. This is where the player is genuinely costing themselves shots, calibrated to their ability level.

**Do not coach from a single round.** One round is noise. Five to ten rounds show a pattern. The Stats page gives you filtered averages.

### Baseline Settings — Important for Elite Players

Every player's SG data is calculated relative to a **benchmark baseline**. By default, this is set automatically from their declared handicap. However, players can manually override it in **Profile → Strokes Gained baseline**.

**Available baselines:** PGA Tour, Scratch, Low amateur (1–9), Mid amateur (10–18), High amateur (19–28), High handicapper (29–36), Beginner.

**For your elite players and plus handicappers:** Encourage them to select the **PGA Tour** baseline. This benchmarks every shot against what a Tour professional would do from the same position. SG values will be negative across the board for even the best amateurs — but that is the point. It shows the precise gap to Tour standard in quantifiable strokes per round per category.

**Example:** A +2 handicapper on the Scratch baseline might show SG: Approach of +0.6 (beating the scratch benchmark). On the Tour baseline, the same player might show −1.8 — losing 1.8 shots per round on approach to Tour professionals. Both numbers are true. Which baseline they use depends on what they are trying to measure.

**Practical guidance for coaches:**
- Club golfers and county players: auto baseline (handicap-derived) is appropriate
- Elite amateurs, county first team, university scholarship players: Scratch or Tour baseline
- Plus-handicap and mini-tour players: Tour baseline recommended

If a player's baseline is wrong, their SG numbers and all coaching feedback will be calibrated to the wrong comparison group. Always check this when onboarding a new player.

### The Magnitude Matters
- **0 to ±0.3:** Within normal variation — not a priority
- **±0.3 to ±0.7:** Meaningful — worth addressing in coaching
- **±0.7 and beyond:** Significant — this is where the player is materially above or below their peers

### Common Coaching Insights from SG Data

**Player losing shots on approach (SG: Approach consistently negative)**  
Look at distance bands in the round detail. The Advanced SG Breakdown (collapsible section on the round detail page) shows SG by distance range: 0–30 yards, 30–75 yards, 75–125 yards, 125–175 yards, 175+ yards. This tells you whether the problem is with a specific club or a specific type of shot.

**Player gaining off tee but losing on approach**  
Classic "driver only" player. Hitting it far enough to attempt challenging approach shots but lacking the iron accuracy to convert. Often benefits from better target selection off the tee rather than technical work.

**Strong approach play but poor putting**  
GIR is high (player is on the green regularly) but putts per hole is high and SG: Putting is negative. Technical putting work or pre-round putting routine focus will have significant impact.

**Consistently losing around the green**  
SG: Around Green is the category most sensitive to short game technique. Bunker play, chipping technique, and pitch shot distance control all feed into this number. This player needs dedicated short game sessions.

## 7. The AI Challenge Feature

The AI Challenge is the most powerful coaching tool on the platform. It is the feature that makes TM Stats uniquely valuable for professional coaches.

### What it Does
The platform generates AI coaching feedback for every player's round. That feedback is based entirely on the statistical data — it doesn't know what you know about the player.

The AI Challenge lets you **add your knowledge** to the analysis. You tell the AI what the stats can't show. The AI then re-analyses the round incorporating your context and produces revised feedback that reflects both the data *and* your coaching insight.

That revised feedback replaces the standard AI feedback on the player's round. They see it labelled "From your coach."

### When to Use It

Use the AI Challenge when you have information that changes the interpretation of the data:

- The player is working on a technical change that will cause short-term regression in a specific area
- The player has an injury affecting certain shots
- The player has a known mental pattern under competition pressure
- The player made strategic decisions (course management) that look bad in stats but were correct given the conditions
- The player is deliberately practising a new shot shape or club selection system

### Step-by-Step

**Step 1: Go to the player's profile**  
From your Coach Dashboard, tap the player's name.

**Step 2: Tap the round you want to challenge**  
From the player's recent rounds list, tap the round you want to add context to.

**Step 3: Open the AI Challenge panel**  
On the round detail page, scroll to the AI Coaching section. You will see the current AI feedback and a button to launch the AI Challenge.

**Step 4: Add your coaching context**  
A text field appears. Write whatever context changes how this round should be interpreted. There is no character limit — be as detailed as you need to be.

**Step 5: Generate revised feedback**  
Tap **Challenge**. The AI re-analyses the round with your context included and generates new feedback. This takes about 10 seconds.

**Step 6: Review and confirm**  
Read the revised feedback. If it accurately reflects the coaching direction you want the player to receive, confirm it. The player will now see this feedback when they look at that round.

### What to Write in the Context Field

Be specific. The more relevant detail you provide, the better the revised feedback will be.

**Good examples:**

> "James is currently working on a new takeaway position we've been drilling for 3 weeks. His iron dispersion will be inconsistent until the movement is automated — this is intentional and expected. The focus should be on the short game, which has been neglected. Please direct the coaching toward around-the-green performance."

> "Sarah played the medal yesterday after a family bereavement. Her mental focus was understandably compromised. The technical performance was actually solid given the circumstances — the double bogeys on 14 and 15 were emotional, not technical. The feedback should acknowledge a resilient technical performance and focus on the positive trend in her ball striking."

> "Tom has been deliberately playing low punch shots off tight lies as a drill we've agreed. His approach numbers will look poor this round — he was hitting punch 7-irons from 170 yards as a practice game. Do not coach the approach play negatively. Focus on the putting, which was genuinely the weak area today."

**Less useful:**

> "He had a bad day."  
> "Normal round."  
> "Ignore the driving stats."

### The Result for Your Player

The revised feedback appears on the player's round detail page with a clear label: **"Your coach has reviewed this round."** It replaces the generic AI feedback with coaching that actually reflects their current programme.

This creates a visible touchpoint between you and every player in your squad, even between sessions.

## 8. Coaching Scenarios and Examples

### Scenario 1: The Improving Junior

**Situation:** A 16-year-old player, 14 handicap, logging rounds regularly. Six weeks of data available.

**What you see in TM Stats:**
- SG: Off Tee improving consistently over 6 weeks (range work is paying off)
- SG: Putting flat (no improvement despite saying they've been practising)
- SG: Around Green strongly negative

**Coaching insight:**  
The range work is working. The putting practice is happening but not translating (possibly a routine or setup issue rather than a stroke issue). Around the green is the biggest opportunity. You now have data to have an honest, evidence-based conversation with the player and their parents.

**AI Challenge use case:**  
The player had a strong competitive round but the AI feedback focused on the putting. You know the putting is being addressed and don't want them to feel disheartened. You add context that the putting is actively being coached and redirect the feedback toward the excellent improvement in ball striking and the short game as the next frontier.

---

### Scenario 2: The Competition Nervous Player

**Situation:** An adult amateur, 8 handicap, consistently scores 4–5 shots better in practice rounds than competitions.

**What you see in TM Stats:**  
Pattern Finder (the AI widget on their dashboard) has already flagged this pattern. Their SG data shows approach play collapses in competitions — specifically from 100–150 yards. Off the tee and putting remain consistent.

**Coaching insight:**  
The distance band data is precise: they are not losing shots everywhere in competitions — they are specifically losing shots with mid-irons under pressure. This is a clear technical trigger point, likely connected to deceleration under pressure with a mid-length swing.

**AI Challenge use case:**  
You add context explaining this is a known pattern and the coaching should focus on a specific pre-shot routine for mid-irons in competition, rather than generic "keep doing what you're doing" advice.

---

### Scenario 3: The Mid-Season Technical Change

**Situation:** A county player, 3 handicap, working with you on a swing change that is temporarily hurting their scores.

**What you see in TM Stats:**  
SG: Approach has gone from +0.3 to −0.6 over the last four rounds. From a data perspective alone, the AI would identify this as a major problem and suggest drilling irons heavily.

**Your knowledge:**  
This regression is entirely expected. The swing change will take 6–8 weeks to automate. Coaching the player to "fix" their approach play right now would undo three months of work.

**AI Challenge use case:**  
Essential. You add detailed context explaining the swing change programme, the expected timeline, and direct the AI to generate feedback that acknowledges the temporary regression, validates the process, and focuses on course management strategies to protect the scorecard while the change beds in.

## 9. Frequently Asked Questions

**Can players see that I've submitted an AI Challenge for their round?**  
Yes. The revised feedback is clearly shown on their round detail page and labelled as coming from their coach. They can see the revised feedback but not your original context input.

**Can I see what AI feedback a player received before I challenge it?**  
Yes. The original AI feedback is displayed in full on the AI Challenge panel before you add any context.

**What if a player hasn't logged any full-tracking rounds?**  
You can still view their basic stats (scores, FIR, GIR, putts) from quick-entry rounds. SG data is only available for full-tracking rounds. Encourage your players to use Full Tracking mode — it takes an extra 2–3 minutes per round and significantly increases the depth of analysis available to both of you.

**Can I remove a player from my squad?**  
Currently players manage their own team membership. A player can leave your team from their Profile settings. Contact info@tmstatsgolf.com if you need a player removed for any reason.

**How many players can I have in my squad?**  
There is no hard limit on squad size.

**Can I have more than one team?**  
Currently one team per coach account. If you need multiple squads (e.g., juniors and adults separately), contact info@tmstatsgolf.com.

**What happens to the AI Challenge feedback if a player upgrades their plan?**  
AI Challenge feedback is stored permanently on the round record. It persists regardless of plan changes.

**I want to give my players Pro access — can I get promo codes?**  
Yes. Contact info@tmstatsgolf.com with the number of players and the duration you need. We will generate promo codes you can distribute.
